## The Problem This Really Solves

AI agents are powerful, however as we can tell _(more now than ever)_ they aren’t fully to be trusted.

They skip specs, tests, and security reviews. They now optimise for saying _“done”_ rather than it being correct.

Ask **Claude Code** or **Cursor** to build a feature, _then ask it to build another one, at times it feels like a guessing game_.

Addy’s repo isn’t trying to replace the agent, it’s giving rules. The same rules that Google follows before shipping features.

## What’s Actually In The Repo

20 skills.

These better prevent agents from trying things like _“I’ll add tests later” or “This is simple enough to skip in the spec”_.

That alone makes this worth taking a look.

**The skills cover:**

-   **Define**, _refine ideas and write specs before a single line of code._
-   **Plan**, split into smaller bite sized chunks/tasks.

## Google Engineering Principles

This isn’t just any workflow, it’s specific principles baked from Google’s engineering culture.

**Hyrum’s Law** _is baked in, if you have enough users, every behaviour of your system is depended on by someone._

**The Beyonce Rule** _is in testing, if you like the outcome, put a test (ring) on it._

**Chesterton’s Fence**, _before removing something you better understand why it was there to begin with._

**Shift Left,** _don’t find bugs after, find them before you even write code._

**Thunk based dev,** _short live branches, small changes, faster merges._

_These principles should be applied to your workflow regardless of if you adopt this setup._

This article discusses the claims surrounding the "smallest 8B model" and critiques the notion of incremental progress in AI. The key takeaway is that the true measure of progress is **Intelligence Density**—how much useful intelligence a model delivers per unit of size and power. True innovation lies in shifting the deployment paradigm from centralized data centers to edge devices by achieving CPU-like efficiency, rather than simply chasing ever-larger parameter counts.

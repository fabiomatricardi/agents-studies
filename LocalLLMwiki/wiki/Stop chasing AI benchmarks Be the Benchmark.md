This article argues that focusing on chasing AI benchmarks is counterproductive. The key takeaway is that users should become the benchmark by defining their own tasks, constraints, and goals, rather than relying solely on external, often misleading, leaderboards. Real progress is found by evaluating models based on real-world impact on daily work and life, rather than narrow, artificial lab conditions.
